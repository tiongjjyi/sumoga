Our Farm Story

Rooted in Port Dickson

Suan Mokkh Garden — meaning "Garden of Liberation" in Thai — was founded in 2013 by the Tiong family on 14 acres of rich highland soil. What began as a modest plot of spinach and herbs has grown into one of Peninsular Malaysia's most trusted certified organic farms.

Every seed carries our commitment: no synthetic fertilisers, no pesticides, no shortcuts. We rotate crops seasonally, enrich soil with organic compost, and harvest by hand so each vegetable reaches you at its absolute peak.

Certified by MyOrganic Malaysia and audited annually. We believe food grown with care should be accessible to everyday Malaysian families.

Our Farm Story

On Symbiosis & The Circle of Life

In our fields, life connects in the most graceful way. We strive to maintain a delicate ecological balance: our lush greenery serves as both a sanctuary for crops and a playground for animals. Here, our sheep roam freely, nourished by the fibrous remnants of our organic harvests. In return, they provide the soil with the richest natural nutrients. This beautiful cycle—sourced from nature and returned to nature— allows the earth to flourish. It is more than just farming; it is the rhythm of life in perfect harmony.

🐑 Grazing Loop

Sheep feed on harvest remnants, reducing field waste.

🌾 Natural Return

Nutrients flow back into soil without synthetic input.

🦋 Shared Habitat

Crops, animals, and pollinators thrive side by side.

♻️ Living Rhythm

Nature-sourced, nature-returned, season after season.

Taste & Flavor: More Than Just Subjective

Higher Dry Matter Content: Because organic vegetables typically have a longer growth cycle, they often contain less water and more dry matter. This results in a denser texture and a more concentrated, robust flavor.

Reduced Pesticide Residue

Clean Eating: Organic farming strictly prohibits the use of synthetic pesticides and herbicides. Choosing organic significantly lowers your body's pesticide load, especially beneficial for children and pregnant women.

🐝

Ecosystem Integrity

Protecting Pollinators: Many synthetic pesticides (such as neonicotinoids) have been linked to bee population collapse. Organic farms avoid these chemicals, providing a safer haven for bees, butterflies, and birds.

🪱

Boosting Biodiversity

Living Soil: By using compost, green manure, and crop rotation, organic farming fosters a thriving underground ecosystem. Organic soil typically contains 30%-60% more earthworms, fungi, and beneficial bacteria.

Garlic Spinach with Sesame & Soft Egg

Fresh baby spinach wilts beautifully in a hot wok with garlic, finished with toasted sesame and a soft-boiled egg. Ready in 12 minutes.

12
Minutes
2
Servings
Easy
Level
  1. 1Boil 2 eggs for 6.5 minutes then transfer to ice water.
  2. 2Heat sesame oil on high. Add 4 cloves minced garlic, sauté 30 seconds.
  3. 3Add 200g baby spinach in batches, toss until just wilted (~90 seconds).
  4. 4Season with light soy and sea salt. Top with sesame seeds and halved eggs.

Star: Cherry Tomatoes

Roasted Cherry Tomato Pasta with Burrata

Slow-roasting concentrates the natural sugars into a jammy, fragrant sauce. Served over pasta with creamy burrata.

35
Minutes
3
Servings
Easy
Level
  1. 1Preheat oven to 200°C. Toss 350g cherry tomatoes with olive oil, garlic, salt.
  2. 2Roast 25–30 minutes until blistered and jammy. Add fresh basil last 2 minutes.
  3. 3Cook 250g pasta al dente. Reserve ½ cup pasta water before draining.
  4. 4Toss pasta with roasted tomatoes and water. Top with torn burrata.

Star: Purple Eggplant

Nyonya-Style Sambal Eggplant

A Malaysian classic — smoky sambal clings to silky eggplant. Bold, fragrant, served with steamed white rice.

25
Minutes
4
Servings
Medium
Level
  1. 1Cut 3 eggplants into wedges, salt lightly, rest 10 minutes.
  2. 2Pan-fry in hot oil until golden on all sides. Set aside.
  3. 3Fry sambal paste with sliced shallots until oil splits.
  4. 4Return eggplant, toss in sambal. Finish with lime. Serve with rice.

Farm-Fresh Nasi Lemak

Creamy coconut rice infused with pandan and lemongrass, plated with sambal, boiled eggs, crunchy peanuts, and fresh cucumber.

Recipe Source: Inspired by Nyonya Cooking's Nasi Lemak recipe and adapted in our own wording/style.
Photo Credit: Wikimedia Commons (Takeaway, CC BY-SA 3.0).

45
Minutes
4
Servings
Medium
Level
  • 2 pandan leaves, knotted We Sell
  • 1 stalk lemongrass, lightly bruised We Sell
  • 4 eggs, boiled We Sell
  • 1 cucumber, sliced We Sell
  • 2 cups rice, rinsed
  • 1 cup coconut milk + 1 cup water
  • Sambal (homemade or ready-made)
  • Toasted peanuts + crispy anchovies
  1. 1Cook rice with coconut milk, water, pandan, lemongrass, and salt until fluffy.
  2. 2Boil eggs to your preferred doneness, then peel and halve.
  3. 3Warm sambal and prep cucumber slices.
  4. 4Plate coconut rice with sambal, eggs, peanuts, anchovies, and cucumber.
  5. 5Finish with extra cucumber and sambal on the side for a fresher farm-style serving.
